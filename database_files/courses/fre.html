French [FRE courses - includes FCS, FSL and JFL478H1]

The following timetable information is the most current information available. Courses and meeting sections added will be listed below. Courses and meeting sections cancelled will be indicated as such.

Students may only register in the course(s) for which they have the stated prerequisites.

The Department reserves the right to place students in the language course appropriate to their level of linguistic competence, based on the results of a placement test. Since 100, 200, 300 and 400-level FSL courses correspond to levels of competence in French, students may be recommended to take a course at a level higher than his/her year of study. The placements, available at www.lang.utoronto.ca/placement/french is mandatory for all students who wish to register in an FRE or FSL course for the first time (except true beginners who have no knowledge of French). The placement test can be take only ONCE and the results of the first test will prevail in the event of several attempts. Ideally, the placement test should be taken in the preceding term of the course students intend to register in, (e.g. for a Fall term or Y course, students should take the placement test in the summer term, prior to their registration date on SWS and before the beginning of classes). Students not having access to a computer capable of managing sound files should write to french.placement@utoronto.ca to arrange to take the test at the Department of French.

For FRE240H1: students must register in each of the following for a total of 4 hours per week: 1 two-hour lecture (L0101) and one two-hour tutorial (T0101, T0102, T0201, T0202).

For FRE272H1: students must register in each of the following for a total of 3 hours per week: 1 two-hour lecture (L0101) and one tutorial (T0101, T0201, T0301 or T0401).

For FRE274H1: students must register in each of the following for a total of 3 hours per week: 1 two-hour lecture (L5101) and one tutorial (T0101, T0201).

For 'E' indicator courses: Applicants must complete the Ballot for Admission and the Description of Project Form, available at www.french.utoronto.ca/courses/145 and return them, duly signed by their supervisor, to Linda Lamisong, room 226, Odette Hall. An electronic version of the application must be forwarded to french.undergraduate@utoronto.ca. The deadlines are as follows: August 30, 2013 for 'F' and 'Y' courses; December 6, 2013 for 'S' courses; April 7, 2014 for the 2014 summer session.
