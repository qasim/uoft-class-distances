Woodsworth College Courses [WDW courses - includes Academic Bridging Program]

The following timetable information is the most current information available. Courses and meeting sections added will be listed below. Courses and meeting sections cancelled will be indicated as such.

For WDW101Y1, WDW102Y1, WDW103Y1, 'E' indicator courses: These courses are available only to students in the Academic Bridging Program. Students must enrol in these courses in person at Woodsworth College, 119 St. George Street.

'S' section code courses run from January 6 to June 13, 2014.

Departmental Hours (September to June): Monday, Wednesday and Thursday, 10:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.; Tuesday, 10:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m.; and Friday, 10:00 a.m. to 4:00 p.m.

Woodsworth ONE:
WDW151H1 and WDW152H1 Lectures 0101, 0201 and 0301 are restricted to students in their first year of undergraduate studies (St. George campus) who have been admitted to Woodsworth One.
To be considered for enrolment, email one.wdw@utoronto.ca
Website: www.wdw.utoronto.ca

WDW151H1 and WDW152H1 Lecture 2001 is restricted to students in the full-time Academic Bridging Program. For assistance please email Academic Bridging or visit the office at 119 St. George Street, Room 220B.

Criminology, Employment Relations
Courses with 'P' enrolment indicator: Students enrolled in an approved subject post are given registration priority. These posts are listed in the enrolment controls column in the timetable below. Remaining spaces will be made available, on ROSI, on the following dates: August 8 for St. George campus degree students; August 13 for other Faculties on the St. George campus and August 15 for Non-degree students on the St. George campus and UTM-UTSC students.

'R'& 'E' indicator courses: students must be enrolled in an approved subject post. These posts are listed in the enrolment controls column in the timetable. It is not possible to accommodate non-program students in these courses. Instructions and application deadlines for 'E' indicator courses are available on the Program website, www.wdw.utoronto.ca

For WDW446H1: This seminar will include a compulsory learning format component using a service learning placement with a community-based agency or organization where students contribute a number of hours per week (usually 2 to 3 hours) to an agency or organization that she/he has been matched to.
