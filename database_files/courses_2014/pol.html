The following timetable information is the most current information available. Courses and meeting sections added will be listed below. Courses and meeting sections cancelled will be indicated as such.

Prerequisites and Exclusions:

  • All POL 100-series courses are mutually exclusive. Students who have previously completed a POL 100-series course with a grade of at least 50% will be removed from POL101Y1 at any time they are discovered.
  • Prerequisites and exclusions are enforced for all political science courses.
  • Prerequisites are not checked at the time of enrolment so do not assume that being able to register in a course means that you satisfy the prerequisites. However prerequisites will be checked by the Department regularly and students enrolled in courses for which they do not have the published prerequisites may have their registration in those courses cancelled at any time.
  • Please consult the Faculty of Arts and Science Calendar or Departments website for details on prerequisites.
  • Students using a course from another institution as a prerequisite must submit a transcript to the Undergraduate Assistant, Sidney Smith Hall 3032 or undergrad.polsci@utoronto.ca before enrolling in the course. Failure to do so will result in removal from the course.
  • Requests for prerequisite waivers will only be considered by the instructor for students in extenuating circumstances. To request a prerequisite waiver, complete a waiver form available at http://politics.utoronto.ca/undergraduate/forms/. The waiver form must be approved by the instructor and filed with the Undergraduate Assistant.

Tutorials: In courses where applicable, tutorials will be arranged after classes commence.


4th year POL Majors and Specialists enrolment in POL 400-level courses:

  • Registration in POL 400-level and Joint POL 400-level courses will be based on initial preferences
  • Further details on listing preferences will be posted at the end of May/early June on the Departments website at http://politics.utoronto.ca/undergraduate/ and the Faculty of Arts and Sciences website at http://artsandscience.utoronto.ca/ofr/timetable/winter/pol.html.
  • Students who are eligible to list their preferences will also be informed via e-mail
  • POL majors are strongly advised to request POL493H1 or POL494H1 as their first choice in their initial preferences
  • There are limited spaces for majors in all other POL 400-level courses
  • Students who have completed the required number of POL 400-level courses needed for their POL program may not necessarily be assigned a space. Specialists need 1.0 FCEs; Majors need 0.5 FCEs
  • Nearly all POL 400-level courses are offered as joint undergraduate-graduate seminars with class sizes ranging from 15-25

POL 400-level Waiting Lists: Students who are more than #3 on the waitlist are unlikely to be able to enroll in the course and may want to secure a space in another course.


For POL 495Y/496H/497H - Independent Studies, 'E' indicator courses: Instructions and application forms are available on the Departments website http://politics.utoronto.ca/undergraduate/forms/.


For all other E indicator courses: Contact the Student and Alumni/Advancement Coordinator, Elizabeth Jagdeo at e.jagdeo@utoronto.ca for details.
