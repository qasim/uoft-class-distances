East Asian Studies [EAS courses]

The following timetable information is the most current information available since the publication of the 2012-2013 Registration Handbook. Courses and meeting sections added will be listed below. Courses and meeting sections cancelled will be indicated as such.

For assistance students may call 416-946-3625 for general information or see the Program Administrator of the Department of East Asian Studies, Robarts Library, Room 14087, 130 St. George Street, Monday to Friday, 10:00 a.m. to 12:00 p.m. and 2:00 to 4:00 p.m.

FOR ALL FIRST YEAR LANGUAGE COURSES the instructor reserves the right to remove students from the class who are found to have inappropriate prior knowledge of the language.

FOR ALL SECOND TO FOURTH YEAR COURSES the instructor will remove students who do not have the proper prerequisites.

CHINESE LANGUAGE COURSES: Any student who wishes to enroll in any modern Chinese language course must undergo a placement interview. Students will be asked to answer questions about their background and their previous experience in language learning. Students will be interviewed on a first-come-first-served basis. Interviews for a course will stop once the course is full. You must bring two placement interview forms to your interview. Go to http://eir.library.utoronto.ca/easic/login.cfm (username: Chinese; password: 2012/2013) and download these forms. Fill them out and bring them to your interview.

CHINESE LANGUAGE COURSES INTERVIEW SCHEDULE: For EAS100Y1, EAS101Y1, 200Y1, EAS201Y1, EAS300Y1 and EAS400Y1: students must have their interview in one of the following two sessions: Thursday, April 5, 2012, 1:00 to 4:00 p.m. or Tuesday, September 4, 2012, 1:00 to 4:00 p.m., Robarts Library, Room 14213. Students who need to be further assessed will take the Chinese language placement tests on Friday, September 7, 2012.
CHINESE LANGUAGE WRITTEN PLACEMENT TESTS: Chinese language written placement tests take place twice a year. In the academic year 2012-2013, placement tests will be held on Friday, September 7, 2012 and Friday, March 1, 2013. Students who wish to take the Chinese language written placements must hand in their registration forms at least two weeks in advance. Please go to http://eir.library.utoronto.ca/easic/login.cfm (username: Chinese; password: 2012/2013) and download these forms.

KOREAN LANGUAGE COURSES: All students who wish to enroll in any modern Korean language course (EAS110Y1, EAS210Y1, EAS310Y1, EAS410Y1) are subject to a placement interview. After a student passes an interview, the Department of East Asian Studies will attempt to register him/her via ROSI. Placement interviews will be held in RL14217 on April 9 (12:00 to 5:00 pm) and September 5 (12:00 to 5:00 pm). Choose a day and bring the placement interview form to your interview. Go to the EASIC website at http://link.library.utoronto.ca/easic/login.cfm to download the interview form (username: Korean; password: 2012/2013). The placement test will be given to students who need further assessment based on the results of the placement interview. Individual students will be notified after the interview as to the location and time of the test. After a student takes the test and is placed in a course, the Department of East Asian Administrator will attempt to register him/her via ROSI.

JAPANESE LANGUAGE COURSES: All newly admitted students (which does not apply to those who repeat the first year) MUST attend the interview held in RL 14147 from 9:00 a.m. to 12:00 p.m. on Tuesday, August 28, 2012. The interview date will not be rescheduled for any reason. (Interview form is available at http://link.library.utoronto.ca/easic/login.cfm (username: Japanese, password: 20122013). Returning students (i.e., students who started university before September 2012) who satisfy the prerequisites to enroll in Japanese language courses may do so through SWS during the first round of enrolment (this applies only to courses with the enrolment indicator 'PE'). If you fail to do so, please go to the EASIC website at http://link.library.utoronto.ca/easic/login.cfm(username: Japanese, password: 20122013) in order to learn how to sign up for the departmental waitlist during the second enrolment round.
Placement Test: The following students must register and take the placement test. Anyone who fails to take the placement test may be removed from Japanese language courses by the Department.
1. Those who have prior background in Japanese
2. Those who do not satisfy the prerequisites
3. Those who want to take EAS121H1S
Deadline for registration: 5:00 p.m., Tuesday, August 21, 2012. Registration form is available at http://link.library.utoronto.ca/easic/login.cfm (username: Japanese, password: 20122013).
Date of the test: Wednesday, August 29, 2012 Time: 10:15 am to 10:45 am (Check-in), 11:00 am to 1:00 pm (Test)
Location: TBA (It will be posted at the EASIC website (same as above) closer to the date of the test.

NON-LANGUAGE COURSES WITH E ENROLMENT INDICATORS: Students should fill out an application form (available on the EAS website), and send to, natasja.vanderberg@utoronto.ca. The Department of East Asian Studies will enroll, via ROSI, all accepted students according to the Art and Science degree students enrolment period.

STUDENTS WHO WANT TO DROP OUT OF ANY COURSE WITH AN 'E' INDICATOR must send an email to natasja.vanderberg@utoronto.ca. Requests should include the course number, section and student name and number. For any other inquiries, please contact the instructors directly by e-mail (address are available on the EAS website).

For EAS103H1 and EAS105H1: If enrolment is high, additional tutorials will be added. Please see the East Asian Departmental Website: http://www.eas.utoronto.ca/ for details.

Departmental Hours: (September 4 to 7) Monday to Friday, 10:00 a.m. to 12:00 p.m. and 2:00 to 4:00 p.m.
